Iran

• What bulls are saying : the escalatory options facing Trump are so bad (destroying Iran’s civilian infrastructure, militarily reopening Hormuz, and/or seizing the country’s enriched uranium) that none of them are likely , especially since the bulk of American strategic objectives have been achieved (the administration will be forced to seek out exit ramps lest it preside over a quagmire ). Hormuz isn’t completely shut , as multiple countries strike deals with Iran to ensure safe passage through the waterway ( Iraq was granted permission over the weekend , a significant development since that country was shipping more than 3 M BPD via Hormuz before the war). Trump sounded somewhat hopeful on Monday about the state of negotiations with Iran and expressed optimism that a deal can be reached before the deadline on Tues at 8pm ET. Iran submitted a 10-point deal proposal to Washington, demanding a guarantee not to be attacked again, an end to the Lebanon war, and a termination of sanctions (this was discussed in a NYT report ). According to Axios , mediators from Pakistan, Egypt, and Turkey are working to broker an agreement (if Trump sees a deal coming together, he will likely push out the 4/7 8pm ET deadline ).
• What bears are saying : the situation in Iran remains on the cusp of catastrophe as all the escalatory options available to Trump risk embroiling the Pentagon in a protracted ground campaign involving tens of thousands of troops and/or triggering a (further) massive economic fallout , with Tehran likely to intensify its campaign of terror against critical assets and infrastructure in the Middle East. Trump sounds very serious about launching an aggressive air campaign aimed at destroying Iran’s civil infrastructure , and media reports (including a WSJ article on Monday) continue to suggest this is a very real possibility . Iran rejected the idea of a 45-day ceasefire and demanded a permanent end to the war . According to a WSJ report Mon night, hopes are fading for a deal to be reached by the Tues night deadline . Deescalating is easier said than done with Iran’s ability and willingness to retaliate throughout the region proving more durable than anticipated while Israel’s war against Hezbollah in Lebanon intensifies and the Houthis become more involved (there are also questions about Iran’s remaining missile stockpiles – some reports suggest its arsenal and launching infrastructure remain substantial ). Even if the war completely ends tomorrow, the global economy will still be forced to grapple with a powerful and extended pulse of stagflationary pressure triggered by the multi-week stretch of fighting. Hormuz is never going back to the way it was before – a significant risk premium will be applied to all commodities and goods flowing through the waterway .
