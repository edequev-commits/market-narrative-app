Iran

• Trump issued a social media post at ~4:10pm ET in which he said the ceasefire deadline has been postponed indefinitely (it was expected to expire Wed night). After insisting in recent days that the deadline would not be moved, Trump stayed true to his word – he eliminated it instead (please note the sarcasm in the preceding sentence). As has been clear for weeks, Trump is stuck – he has no good options to kinetically escalate (which is why he continues not to do so), he can’t simply exit , and the status quo (with Hormuz shut) isn’t tenable , and as a result, his only realistic path forward is pushing for some type of a “deal” , and it’s likely one arrives in the relatively near term (we put “deal” in quotes as whatever gets announced won’t be much different than a grudging, nonbinding détente between the two sides with some deliverables but nothing that solves the core problems in perpetuity ). While there is still a bit of skepticism and cynicism in the market about Iran , most are of the view that Operation Epic Fury is past its peak , with an agreement of some sort more likely than not .

### **The Quiet Power of Consistent Saving**

When people think about building wealth, they often imagine high-paying jobs, lucky investments, or sudden windfalls. Yet, one of the most reliable and underrated strategies is consistent saving. It may not feel glamorous, but over time, it becomes one of the most powerful financial habits a person can develop.

Saving consistently is less about how much you put aside and more about the discipline behind it. Even small amounts—set aside regularly—can grow into meaningful sums. This works because of accumulation and, when invested, compounding. The earlier someone starts, the less they need to rely on large contributions later in life.

One key advantage of consistent saving is financial resilience. Life is unpredictable, and unexpected expenses are inevitable. Having savings provides a buffer that prevents reliance on debt. Without savings, even minor financial setbacks can spiral into long-term problems. With savings, those same setbacks become manageable inconveniences.

Another benefit is psychological. Knowing you have money set aside creates a sense of security and control. This reduces stress and allows for better decision-making. People who save regularly are less likely to make impulsive financial choices because they are already thinking long-term.

Automation plays a major role in successful saving. By setting up automatic transfers to a savings account, individuals remove the need for constant decision-making. This reduces the temptation to spend and ensures consistency. Over time, saving becomes a habit rather than a chore.

• Space X announced after the close plans to buy Cursor later this year for $60 B (or pay Cursor a $10 B fee for the work being done together) in a deal that would bring together x AI and Cursor to create “the world’s best coding and knowledge work AI” . What does this mean? While AI chatbots like Chat GPT get a lot of attention, the real area of focus for the industry in terms of monetizing all the capex are coding tools (which are used not only for quickly writing software, but also creating, deploying, and managing agents), with Anthropic’s Claude Code the highest-profile product followed by Open AI’s Codex and Cursor. Musk is making a big pitch on space-based data centers , but the AI part of the Space X story is dubious at best , with x AI trailing behind the industry’s leaders in terms of market and mindshare, but the addition of Cursor makes the investment thesis much more compelling , which should help the upcoming IPO. $60 B is a lot of money, but if Cursor helps Space X achieve the upper end of its valuation goals (i.e. $2 T), it would be money well spent.
• Space X will warn in its IPO documents that plans for space-based data centers may not be commercially viable ( Reuters )
