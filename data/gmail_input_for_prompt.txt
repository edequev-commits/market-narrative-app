HOW WE'RE THINKING ABOUT MARKETS:
How we’re thinking about markets – while it’s still early in the CQ1 reporting season , and the full fallout from the Iran war hasn’t been felt yet in the economy , we’ve been positively surprised by corporate results thus far , especially the “status quo” messaging from bank CEOs (which have the best insight into macro conditions of any industry). In addition, economic activity (growth and inflation) hasn’t been disrupted as much as feared by the war , at least so far. However, with the SPX up ~11%+ on a MTD basis and the index in overbought territory , the time to aggressively buy has come to an end, at least in the immediate term . For the last 1.5 weeks we’ve felt that an Iran détente/deal was much more likely than not , but this is now becoming the consensus view , which means all the “a deal is close”-type of headlines will lose their ability to rally the market . Finally, the economy won’t escape operation Epic Fury without any impact – the effects of the war will be stagflationary , likely (at least) into the summer or fall .

Iran

• What bulls are talking about : all signs point to the US and Iran heading toward a sustainable détente , if not a full deal . Reports suggest the US and Iran could extend the current ceasefire for an additional two weeks (to allow more time for negotiations), while Israel contemplates a short-term ceasefire in Lebanon . Iran could permit all ships to sail freely through Hormuz on the Oman side of the waterway according to Reuters (this would both unlock the Strait and allow Iran to say it still “controls” the shipping lane, albeit just one side of it ). Trump seems to be pivoting aggressively away from kinetic escalation (it’s very likely that the bombing campaign won’t resume, and a large-scale troop deployment is extremely improbable).
• What bears are talking about : Trump remains in an Iran pickle , unable to escalate militarily or fully exit . The blockade could trigger an unexpected military confrontation , causing an immediate resumption of the war (a Washington Post report Wed morning said the Pentagon is still sending additional troops and resources into the Middle East). Iran issued a warning on Wed that it would target Red Sea shipping if the Hormuz blockade persisted . The stagflationary economic fallout from the war , which is only just now seeping into growth and inflation data , will continue for at least the next several months .

Iran

• What bulls are talking about : all signs point to the US and Iran heading toward a sustainable détente , if not a full deal . Reports suggest the US and Iran could extend the current ceasefire for an additional two weeks (to allow more time for negotiations), while Israel contemplates a short-term ceasefire in Lebanon . Iran could permit all ships to sail freely through Hormuz on the Oman side of the waterway according to Reuters (this would both unlock the Strait and allow Iran to say it still “controls” the shipping lane, albeit just one side of it ). Trump seems to be pivoting aggressively away from kinetic escalation (it’s very likely that the bombing campaign won’t resume, and a large-scale troop deployment is extremely improbable).
• What bears are talking about : Trump remains in an Iran pickle , unable to escalate militarily or fully exit . The blockade could trigger an unexpected military confrontation , causing an immediate resumption of the war (a Washington Post report Wed morning said the Pentagon is still sending additional troops and resources into the Middle East). Iran issued a warning on Wed that it would target Red Sea shipping if the Hormuz blockade persisted . The stagflationary economic fallout from the war , which is only just now seeping into growth and inflation data , will continue for at least the next several months .
