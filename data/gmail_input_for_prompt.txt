Iran

• Trump at 6:30pm ET Tues night (about 90 minutes prior to his 8pm ET deadline) said the US would suspend its bombing campaign against Iran for a period of two weeks . This is contingent on Iran agreeing to the “complete, immediate, and safe opening” of Hormuz .
• Trump says he’s agreeing to a 14-day ceasefire because the US has “met and exceeded” all its military objectives and is “very far along” on a “definitive agreement” for “long term peace with Iran” . Trump goes on to claim that “ almost all of the various points of past contention ” between the US and Iran have been resolved, and the 2-week ceasefire will allow for an agreement “to be finalized and consummated” . The 10-point plan submitted by Iran is a “workable basis” on which to negotiate according to Trump.
• Iran has approved the 2-week proposal according to media reports , including safe passage through Hormuz , although it’s not entirely clear whether it will impose fees on ships (Tehran issued a somewhat celebratory response , claiming that it forced Washington to accept its 10-point plan ). It looks like Israel will abide by the ceasefire in Iran (although Netanyahu said it doesn’t apply to Lebanon ).
• According to Axios, the US and Iran will hold negotiations starting this Friday in Islamabad, and there is a lot of difficult negotiating to be done (on enrichment, nuclear material, missiles, reparations and/or Hormuz tolls, proxy groups, sanctions, etc.). Meanwhile, the Iran regime didn’t only survive , but it’s now embittered .
• Our view for a while has been that since Trump faced only horrible escalatory options from here (destroying civilian infrastructure, militarily reopening Hormuz, and/or seizing the enriched uranium), since Iran obtained an ability and showed a willingness to wage a campaign of economic terror throughout the region , since the global economy was already buckling under the weight of a powerful burst of stagflationary pressure from the last 5 weeks of fighting (this is still going to pass through the data and company EPS for at least the next several months), since political opposition was growing in Washington (it’s doubtful a $200 B supplemental would have passed Congress, even via reconciliation), and since the US has achieved the bulk of its strategic objectives (degrading Iran’s nuclear and missile capabilities), an offramp was the more likely outcome .
• The Iraq I scenario looks increasingly likely, which will likely require an enhanced US military presence to stay in the Middle East for years working to contain Tehran (this is much better than Iraq II , which would have meant a multi-year quagmire draining the US of both blood and money).
• A 2-week ceasefire is obviously positive news, and stocks will celebrate , but keep in mind the SPX was struggling with the 6900-7000 level before the war, so that is likely to be even stronger resistance now . The economy (and Corporate America) are going to be forced to grapple with the fallout from the last 5 weeks ( this will be a months-long process, if not longer ). The FOMC is (at best) on the sidelines for the balance of the year, and the pace of Fed asset purchases will slide materiall y after this month (while the ECB, BOE, and BOJ are all likely to tighten ).
• Stocks that benefited from the war (energy, commodity chemicals, defense contractors, etc.) will probably suffer aggressive profit taking while consumer discretionary (including housing-linked names) should see the biggest rally .
• Hormuz will never go back to the way it was before – Iran’s ability to shut the waterway will embed a risk premium in the price of all commodities flowing through it for the foreseeable future (there will be an accelerated process of constructing infrastructure such that countries, including Saudi Arabia, can bypass Hormuz , but this will take years to come online ).
• The NYT on Tuesday published a long profile article detailing Trump’s decision to launch the Iran war – the main takeaways in our view were 1) the amount of opposition that existed inside the White House to the campaign (Vance, Caine, and Rubio were all opposed to varying degrees) and 2) the degree of concern inside the Pentagon about how a conflict would deplete munition stockpiles to dangerously low levels (overall, the report suggests the White Houde doesn’t plan on sustaining a protracted campaign ).
