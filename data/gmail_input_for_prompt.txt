Iran

• What bulls are talking about : it isn’t just that Trump (seemed to) extend the ceasefire in perpetuity , but by doing so he strongly demonstrated (once again) an extreme reluctance to escalate kinetically , removing a major risk from the table. Based on all the media reporting in the last 48 hours, it seems that if the US lifted its blockade for a brief period (even just a few days), Iran would sit down in Pakistan , and a deal could come together quickly (it appears that the two sides have narrowed their differences meaningfully on the key issues). Pakistani officials sounded hopeful (according to the NYT) about a US-Iranian negotiation happening within the next few days (Trump himself told the NY Post that a meeting might happen soon ). Trump thanked the Iranian leadership on social media (at ~1pm ET on Wed) for not proceeding with the planned execution of 8 female protesters. Karoline Leavitt said Trump doesn’t consider Iran’s seizure of two ships in Hormuz to be a violation of the ceasefire .
• What bears are talking about : Trump seems stuck in Iran , unwilling to escalate militarily , unable to exit completely , and forced to impose a Hormuz blockade that’s economically untenable for an extended period. The current Hormuz standoff is increasingly a tinderbox , creating the risk of unplanned escalation (the US and Iran are both interdicting ships, and the situation could easily spiral). The Israel/Lebanon ceasefire , which is critical to the US-Iran détente , feels particularly tenuous. The best-case scenario remains a vague MOU (not a final, absolute, and timeless deal) that reduces tensions temporarily but fails to address the long-term drivers of the present conflict. Even if Operation Epic Fury is past its kinetic peak (which isn’t guaranteed), the fallout from the conflict will be significantly stagflationary for several quarters (at least), and supply chains in the region will never fully return to pre-war status (anything flowing through Hormuz will be subject to a material risk premium going forward). According to a Pentagon assessment cited in a Washington Post article , it might take as long as 6 months once the war formally ends to fully clear Hormuz of mines. The head of the IEA reiterated his warning that the world faces the biggest energy shock in history because of what’s happening in the Middle East.

Party Poopers

American money is green, not red or blue, but that isn’t how some people invest theirs. A clear partisan tinge affects views of stocks and the economy.

In September 2024, for example, two months before the presidential election, the long-running University of Michigan survey of consumer sentiment topped 70—a decent report. But the number was an abysmal 49.7 among self-identified Republicans and a stellar 92.6 among Democrats. That flipped as soon as President Trump was re-elected.

A less-frequent Gallup survey specifically about whether the stock market will rise in the next six months hewed to the same pattern. This January, 81% of Republicans, but just 35% of Democrats, thought it would. Those numbers were 22% and 43%, respectively, two years earlier.

Has there actually been a difference in stock-market performance based on which party occupied the White House? For what it’s worth, yes. Returns on average have been far lower under supposedly pro-business Republicans.
