Iran

• What bulls are talking about : nearly all of the headlines out of the Middle East point to an easing of acrimony and tensions as the US and Iran head toward an extension of the current ceasefire and an eventual nuclear/Hormuz deal . Trump said the war “should be ending pretty soon” and claimed that Iran has agreed to give up its enriched uranium and abandoned ambitions to build a nuclear weapon . In addition, Israel and Lebanon will observe a 10-day ceasefire.
• What bears are talking about : it’s now overwhelmingly the consensus view that the US and Iran will reach some sort of a deal to resolve their conflict and reopen Hormuz , which means headlines pointing to an agreement won’t place as much upside pressure on stocks as they did before (and the fallout from more acrimony will be significant). A durable détente isn’t guaranteed – Washington and Tehran still have some big differences to iron out , as do Israel and Lebanon . According to a Bloomberg report on Thurs, some Gulf and European governments believe it will take the US and Iran about 6 months to reach a deal , a far longer timeframe than is suggested by the current market narrative. A Reuters article highlighted the negative impact to US munitions supplies from the war , which will force the administration to delay certain weapons deliveries to Europe . Even if a deal is reached , it won’t erase the fallout from the war – the stagflationary effects of Epic Fury will be with markets for a while. The big price increase announced from PPG Wed night won’t be an isolated incident – companies across the board will be forced to adjust pricing given what’s happening in commodities, and ~$80-95 is likely to be the “new normal” for Brent vs. ~$60-70 before the war.

Iran

• What bulls are talking about : nearly all of the headlines out of the Middle East point to an easing of acrimony and tensions as the US and Iran head toward an extension of the current ceasefire and an eventual nuclear/Hormuz deal . Trump said the war “should be ending pretty soon” and claimed that Iran has agreed to give up its enriched uranium and abandoned ambitions to build a nuclear weapon . In addition, Israel and Lebanon will observe a 10-day ceasefire.
• What bears are talking about : it’s now overwhelmingly the consensus view that the US and Iran will reach some sort of a deal to resolve their conflict and reopen Hormuz , which means headlines pointing to an agreement won’t place as much upside pressure on stocks as they did before (and the fallout from more acrimony will be significant). A durable détente isn’t guaranteed – Washington and Tehran still have some big differences to iron out , as do Israel and Lebanon . According to a Bloomberg report on Thurs, some Gulf and European governments believe it will take the US and Iran about 6 months to reach a deal , a far longer timeframe than is suggested by the current market narrative. A Reuters article highlighted the negative impact to US munitions supplies from the war , which will force the administration to delay certain weapons deliveries to Europe . Even if a deal is reached , it won’t erase the fallout from the war – the stagflationary effects of Epic Fury will be with markets for a while. The big price increase announced from PPG Wed night won’t be an isolated incident – companies across the board will be forced to adjust pricing given what’s happening in commodities, and ~$80-95 is likely to be the “new normal” for Brent vs. ~$60-70 before the war.

Iran

• What bulls are talking about : nearly all of the headlines out of the Middle East point to an easing of acrimony and tensions as the US and Iran head toward an extension of the current ceasefire and an eventual nuclear/Hormuz deal . Trump said the war “should be ending pretty soon” and claimed that Iran has agreed to give up its enriched uranium and abandoned ambitions to build a nuclear weapon . In addition, Israel and Lebanon will observe a 10-day ceasefire.
• What bears are talking about : it’s now overwhelmingly the consensus view that the US and Iran will reach some sort of a deal to resolve their conflict and reopen Hormuz , which means headlines pointing to an agreement won’t place as much upside pressure on stocks as they did before (and the fallout from more acrimony will be significant). A durable détente isn’t guaranteed – Washington and Tehran still have some big differences to iron out , as do Israel and Lebanon . According to a Bloomberg report on Thurs, some Gulf and European governments believe it will take the US and Iran about 6 months to reach a deal , a far longer timeframe than is suggested by the current market narrative. A Reuters article highlighted the negative impact to US munitions supplies from the war , which will force the administration to delay certain weapons deliveries to Europe . Even if a deal is reached , it won’t erase the fallout from the war – the stagflationary effects of Epic Fury will be with markets for a while. The big price increase announced from PPG Wed night won’t be an isolated incident – companies across the board will be forced to adjust pricing given what’s happening in commodities, and ~$80-95 is likely to be the “new normal” for Brent vs. ~$60-70 before the war.
